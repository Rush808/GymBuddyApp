A bar squat is an important excercise to increase strength in the lower body, core, and back. Most importantly, it targets your hamstring and glute muscles. A bar squat may be a difficult excersie to start due to poor bar placement on your back, this can result to uncomfortable bar positioning. But with lots of practice, you can master a bar squat.

  • Position the bar just below the spine of the scapula (that bony ridge bewlow your traps).
  • Reach back and place your hands on the bar and make it level to your shoulers.
  • Unrack and step back from the rack while positioning your feet to prepare for a squat.
  • line your back straight while you squat down.
  • Then, come back up by driving your heel to the floor
